Portfolio Optimisation (Finance)

Constructed efficient frontiers using CAPM-based expected returns and compared allocation strategies including mean–variance, risk-parity, and equal-weight benchmarks. Backtested portfolios with rolling windows to evaluate Sharpe ratios, drawdowns, and stability, highlighting the trade-offs between risk and return.

  • GitHub

Python · Quantitative Finance · Optimisation · Risk Analysis · Data Science

Overview

The project demonstrates quantitative finance techniques to build and evaluate diversified portfolios. Using historical returns, I estimated expected return, risk, and covariance matrices to simulate allocation strategies. The focus was on risk-adjusted performance, stability, and reproducibility.

  • Task: Asset allocation and efficient frontier construction.
  • Assets: Equity ETFs (replace with your tickers, e.g., SPY, QQQ, EFA).
  • Methods: CAPM, mean–variance optimisation, risk parity, naive equal-weight.
  • Stack: Python, pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, cvxpy, matplotlib.

Data & Features

  • Inputs: Daily adjusted close prices from Yahoo Finance (via yfinance API).
  • Returns: log returns, monthly aggregation for stability.
  • Covariance estimation: shrinkage (Ledoit-Wolf) vs sample covariance.
  • Expected returns: CAPM with market factor, or historical mean.
  • Constraints: no short-selling, weights sum to 1.

Optimisation Methods

Three portfolio construction methods were compared:

  • Mean–variance optimisation: maximise Sharpe ratio subject to constraints.
  • Risk parity: allocate such that each asset contributes equally to portfolio risk.
  • Naive equal-weight: benchmark baseline (1/N allocation).

Results & Insights

  • Efficient frontier showed significant diversification benefits over single-asset choices.
  • Mean–variance portfolios achieved highest Sharpe but were less stable across rolling windows.
  • Risk parity allocations offered smoother performance and lower drawdowns.
  • Naive equal-weight surprisingly competitive, underscoring difficulty of beating simple baselines.

Limitations

  • CAPM assumes stable factor relationships, which may not hold across regimes.
  • Backtests ignore transaction costs and rebalancing friction.
  • Limited to equities; multi-asset expansion would improve diversification realism.

Reproducibility & Packaging

  • Config-driven pipeline: fetch → clean → compute returns → optimise → backtest.
  • Stored config files (tickers, constraints) and saved frontier plots for each run.
  • Modular scripts + Jupyter notebook for exploration.

Next Steps

  • Add bonds/commodities to broaden diversification.
  • Incorporate robust covariance estimators (e.g., Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage, factor models).
  • Cost-aware optimisation including turnover constraints.
  • Deploy interactive dashboard (Plotly Dash/Streamlit) for scenario analysis.
