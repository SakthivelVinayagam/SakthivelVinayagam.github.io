Laptop Review Topic Modelling (NLP)

Applied unsupervised NLP on consumer laptop reviews to extract themes such as battery life, performance, and value for money. Compared LDA with BERTopic for topic coherence and interpretability, then built visualisations (heatmaps, sentiment by topic) to reveal brand-specific strengths and weaknesses.

  • Case Study (PDF)
  • GitHub

Python · NLP · Topic Modelling · BERTopic · LDA

Overview

The goal was to understand what customers discuss most across laptop brands and models, and whether topics align with sentiment (e.g., “battery life” often negative; “keyboard” mixed). I cleaned and deduplicated review text, created document embeddings, and extracted topics with two approaches: LDA (classic bag-of-words) and BERTopic (embedding-based with HDBSCAN).

  • Task: Unsupervised topic discovery + sentiment trend exploration.
  • Scale: (Replace with your count) e.g., ~10–20k review snippets across brands.
  • Outputs: Topic list with top words/examples, brand-topic heatmaps, sentiment by topic.
  • Stack: Python, pandas, scikit-learn, spaCy/NLTK, BERTopic, Gensim (LDA), plotly.

Data & Preprocessing

  • Source: Public e-commerce / review datasets (brand, model, text, rating).
  • Cleaning: lowercasing, punctuation/HTML removal, emoji/URL stripping.
  • Normalisation: lemmatisation; light stopword extension for product terms (e.g., “laptop”, “windows”).
  • De-duplication: remove near-duplicates using cosine similarity threshold.
  • Sentiment: rating-to-polarity mapping (e.g., ≤2 negative, 3 neutral, ≥4 positive) or lexicon-based as fallback.

Methods

I compared topic quality and interpretability across LDA and BERTopic:

  • LDA: vectorised text (TF-IDF / BoW), tuned topic count k, filtered low-frequency terms; measured c_v coherence.
  • BERTopic: transformer embeddings → UMAP dimension reduction → HDBSCAN clustering → c-TF-IDF per topic; measured topic diversity and coherence.
  • Labelling: manual topic names from top words + exemplar documents.

Key Visualisations

  • Topic prevalence bar chart (BERTopic/LDA) with representative n-grams.
  • Brand × Topic heatmap to compare focus areas (e.g., gaming brands over-index on thermals/GPUs).
  • Sentiment by topic (stacked bars) to highlight pain points (e.g., battery, fan noise).
  • UMAP 2D embedding plot showing cluster separations and outliers.

Findings

  • Core topics: Battery life, Display/Screen, Performance, Thermals/Fans, Keyboard/Trackpad, Build/Weight, Price/Value.
  • BERTopic produced more coherent, specific topics (better short phrase capture) than LDA at the same k.
  • Sentiment skew: “battery life” and “thermals” tilting negative; “display” and “keyboard” more mixed/positive depending on brand.
  • Brand clusters reflect positioning: e.g., ultrabooks → positive on weight/build, gaming → mixed on thermals/noise.

Limitations

  • Topic labels are human-curated; some overlap between “performance” and “GPU/CPU”.
  • Sentiment from star ratings ≠ true sentiment of the text; sarcasm/nuance can mislead.
  • Sampling bias: review platforms skew toward certain buyer segments.

Reproducibility & Packaging

  • Config-driven pipeline: clean → deduplicate → embed/vectorise → model → charts.
  • Saved topic models, coherence metrics, and labelled topic dictionary.
  • Notebook/Script to regenerate plots and HTML assets.
